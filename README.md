# On Our Terms
**A free, self-paced interactive web course on digital consent and data literacy.**

-----

## What is this?

*On Our Terms* is a free educational web course that teaches everyday users what is actually happening to their data, their attention, and their bodies when they are interacting with today's technology - including but not limited to contemporary social media platforms, algorithmic-based content feeds, and AI-powered chatbots.

This project was originally created as part of an Embedded Ethics Certificate at the University of San Diego. 

It is free. It will always be free.

-----

## The Problem

Most of us have "agreed" to hundreds of Terms of Service without:
- Actually reading them (often, because of their inaccessible language and design).
- Understanding the extent of data that is being collected about us, or how it is used.
- Ever being taught that we have rights surrounding any of this.

This basic gap in the user consent processs hasn't occurred by accident. These are the direct effects of systems which have been designed to take advantage of manufactured ignorance, and are strategically engineered for the benefit of tech companies to:
- Idenitfy exactly how maximize our consumer potential.
- Perform mass surveillance projects.
- Attempt to manipulate our personal beliefs and everyday behavior.

-----

## Who is This For?

*On Our Terms* is built for two primary audiences:

- **Teenagers and young adults** who are now growing up completely immersed in a post-digital society.
- **Parents and older adults** who didn’t grow up with this technology, and haven’t been given the education they deserve about what they’re engaging with.

-----

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, users should be able to:

1. Describe how tech hijacks our own biology to addict us
2. Understand basics surrounding data collection and processing, and how information gathered about us isn't always used for our best interests
3. Feel equipped with specific knowledge & tools to navigate tech in a more informed and intentional way 
4. Know exactly how you can advocate for and use your data rights
